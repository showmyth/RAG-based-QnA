# Artificial Intelligence Questions

1. What is artificial intelligence?
   Artificial intelligence is the broader field of building systems that can perform tasks requiring human-like intelligence, such as reasoning, learning, problem-solving, language understanding, and perception. Machine learning is one subfield of AI.

2. What is the difference between AI and machine learning?
   AI is the broad discipline of making machines act intelligently. Machine learning is a subset of AI where systems learn patterns from data instead of being programmed with explicit rules for every case. Not all AI systems use machine learning.

3. What is a rational agent in AI?
   A rational agent is an entity that chooses actions expected to maximize its performance measure based on what it knows. It perceives its environment through sensors and acts through actuators. Rationality depends on the available information and goals.

4. What are the main types of AI?
   AI is often categorized as narrow AI, general AI, and superintelligent AI. Narrow AI is built for specific tasks and is what we currently have in practice. General AI refers to human-level flexible intelligence, which remains hypothetical.

5. What is knowledge representation?
   Knowledge representation is the way an AI system stores facts, rules, and relationships so it can reason about them. Examples include logic, semantic networks, frames, ontologies, and knowledge graphs. Good representation makes inference more effective.

6. What is a search problem in AI?
   A search problem involves finding a sequence of actions that moves from an initial state to a goal state. AI represents the problem as states, actions, transition rules, and a goal test. Many AI tasks such as pathfinding and puzzle solving use search.

7. What is the difference between uninformed and informed search?
   Uninformed search methods like BFS and DFS do not use domain-specific knowledge beyond the problem definition. Informed search methods such as A* use heuristics to estimate how close a state is to the goal, which can make search more efficient.

8. What is a heuristic?
   A heuristic is an estimate or rule of thumb used to guide problem solving more efficiently. In search algorithms, it helps prioritize promising states. A good heuristic reduces search time while remaining computationally cheap.

9. What is the Turing Test?
   The Turing Test is a classic thought experiment proposed by Alan Turing to evaluate whether a machine can exhibit human-like conversational behavior. If a human evaluator cannot reliably distinguish the machine from a human based on conversation alone, the machine is said to pass the test.

10. What is expert system in AI?
    An expert system is an AI program that uses a knowledge base and inference rules to emulate decision-making by a human expert in a narrow domain. It was one of the earliest successful AI approaches and is useful where rules can be clearly encoded.

11. What is natural language processing?
    Natural language processing, or NLP, is the area of AI that enables computers to understand, interpret, and generate human language. It includes tasks such as translation, summarization, sentiment analysis, and question answering.

12. What is computer vision?
    Computer vision is the branch of AI that enables machines to interpret and analyze visual information from images or videos. Common tasks include image classification, object detection, segmentation, and facial recognition.
